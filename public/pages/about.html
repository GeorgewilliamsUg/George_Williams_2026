Who I Am

I came to the Bible
looking for God.

Not inspiration. Not comfort. Not a framework for better living. I came looking for God, and what I found changed everything.

My name is George and I am married to Prossy. I work with children by day, design websites by night, and help pastors in distant places understand the Bible when time allows.

What you'll find written here comes from a simple, persistent habit: I wonder about almost everything as a Christian. A conversation, a verse read for the hundredth time but suddenly seen differently, a problem at work, a question a child asks without knowing how deep it runs, all of it tends to lodge itself in my mind and refuse to leave until I have turned it over from a few different angles. I ask more questions than I answer, and I have made a kind of peace with that, because Scripture itself seems unafraid of the search. I have come to think that searching, not merely arriving, is often where the real learning happens. So, this space holds whatever intrigue I happen to be chasing at the time: reflections on a passage of Scripture, half-formed thoughts on ministry or technology, observations from ordinary days that turned out to mean more than expected.
