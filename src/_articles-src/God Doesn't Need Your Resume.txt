God Doesn't Need Your Resume

He called the unqualified. He still does.
You were not chosen because of what you brought to the table.
Moses had a stutter and a warrant. Gideon was hiding in a winepress. Jeremiah said he was too young. Peter swore with a fisherman's mouth and denied Christ three times before breakfast. Paul held coats while men stoned believers and called it righteous work.
These are not the footnotes of the story. These are the main characters.
And God chose every single one of them.
The Logic of Grace Is Not Your Logic
We live inside a system that rewards credentials. The right degree. The right network. The right track record. Opportunity flows to the impressive, and we have quietly imported that economy into our reading of Scripture until we have built a God who works the same way.
He does not.
Paul lays it out with surgical precision in 1 Corinthians 1:27: God chose what is foolish in the world to shame the wise; God chose what is weak in the world to shame the strong. This is not a consolation prize for the unimpressive. It is a deliberate strategy. God selects the vessel that cannot possibly take credit for what flows through it, so that when something glorious happens, there is only one place to look.
A.W. Tozer wrote in The Pursuit of God that God is looking for people through whom He can do the impossible. What have you done that is impossible? If the answer involves your own competence, look again.
Gideon's Army and the Math That Makes No Sense
Judges 7 is one of the strangest military briefings in history.
Israel is outnumbered. They need every man they have. And God tells Gideon to send soldiers home until thirty-two thousand become ten thousand, and then ten thousand become three hundred. Three hundred men against an army the text describes as thick as locusts.
Why? Verse 2 gives the reason without apology: lest Israel boast over me, saying, my own hand has saved me.
God was not being reckless. He was being precise. He engineered the odds so that the only explanation left standing after the victory would be Himself. The three hundred were not selected for their excellence. They were selected for His glory.
John Piper has said it across decades of preaching in a hundred different ways, but it always lands in the same place: God does everything He does for His own glory, and our deepest joy is found not in spite of that but because of it. The Gideon story is not about Gideon. It is about a God who wins battles that cannot be won and then hands the unqualified man the sword that is already bloodless after the enemy routed itself in the dark.
Moses and the Burning Bush Conversation
Exodus 3 and 4 contain one of the most honest exchanges in all of Scripture.
God appears in fire and commissions Moses to confront the most powerful man on earth. And Moses, who has been living in obscurity for forty years after a spectacular failure in Egypt, responds with five consecutive objections. Who am I? What if they don't believe me? I am not eloquent. I am slow of speech. Send someone else.
He is not performing humility. He means it. He genuinely cannot see how this ends well.
God's response to every objection is not a rebuke of Moses' weakness. It is a revelation of His own sufficiency. I will be with you. I AM WHO I AM. I will be your mouth. The question God keeps redirecting is not can Moses do this. It is who God is.
John MacArthur, in his commentary on Exodus, notes that God's patience with Moses here is not indulgence. It is instruction. Every objection Moses raises becomes an occasion for God to say: the issue is not your capacity. The issue is my presence.
What This Means for You, George
You are broke. You carry a vision for HopeAbound that is larger than your wallet and wider than your current reach. You are writing articles with a calling that outpaces your platform. You are building toward something you cannot yet see the scaffolding for.
That is a Gideon situation. That is a Moses situation.
The resources are thin by design. Not because God forgot or because the vision was wrong. But because when HopeAbound stands and the articles reach the people they are meant to reach and the community gets built, the arithmetic will not add up in any direction that credits you. And that is the point.
A.W. Pink wrote in Gleanings in Exodus that God deliberately strips a man down before He builds him up, so that the building is unmistakably His work and not the man's renovation project.
You are in the stripping season. It is not the end of the story. It is the preparation for it.
The Only Qualification That Matters
There is one thing that every man and woman God ever used had in common. Not talent. Not education. Not connections. Not even a clean record.
Availability.
Abraham left without knowing where he was going. Mary said be it done to me according to your word without understanding what that would cost her. The disciples left their nets the same afternoon they were asked.
God does not audit your past. He asks about your yes.
So stop waiting until you are ready. You will not be ready. You were not meant to be ready. You were meant to be willing, and then to watch what God does with that willingness when He adds Himself to it.
The resume is irrelevant.
He already knows what He is going to do with you.
