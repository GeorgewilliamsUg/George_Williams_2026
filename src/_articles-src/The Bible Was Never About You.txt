
The Bible Was Never About You
And that is the best news you will ever receive.

We came to the Book looking for ourselves.

We wanted the five steps to a better marriage, the morning routine of David, the leadership secrets of Nehemiah. We wanted a mirror. What we found, if we were honest enough to stay long enough, was a window. And on the other side of that window, filling the entire frame, was not us.

It was Him.

The Story You Walked Into

Open Genesis and you do not meet a life coach. You meet a Creator who speaks light into nothing and calls it good. Turn to the last page of Revelation and the final cry of Scripture is not "you made it" but "Come, Lord Jesus." The whole book, from the garden to the city, from the fall to the restoration, from the bleeding lamb in Exodus to the Lamb upon the throne, is one story moving in one direction toward one Person.

Jesus said it plainly to the Pharisees in John 5:39: you search the Scriptures because you think that in them you have eternal life, and it is they that bear witness about me.

The Bible is not a collection of wisdom literature that happens to mention God occasionally. It is the story of God glorifying Himself through the rescue of people who had no business being rescued. Every character in it is a supporting role. Abraham, Moses, David, even Paul. They are not the point. They are signposts.

What Happens When We Make It About Us

We shrink it.

A God who exists to sort out your Monday morning becomes manageable. Domesticated. The kind of deity you can negotiate with, ignore when inconvenient, and return to when you need something. Joel Osteen's god fits in your pocket. The God of Isaiah does not fit in the universe.

When we turn the Bible into a self-help manual, we do not just misread it. We lose it entirely. We lose the weight of Romans 11, where Paul looks into the depths of God's sovereign mercy and the only response he can produce is worship. We lose the terror of Hebrews 12, the consuming fire. We lose the staggering scandal of grace, because grace is only scandalous when you understand it was not arranged for your comfort but for God's glory, and that somehow, miraculously, your good was bound up in that.

A.W. Tozer wrote that the most important thing about you is what comes to your mind when you think about God. He was right. And most of us, if we are truthful, think about a God who is primarily concerned with our flourishing. The Bible thinks about a God who is primarily concerned with His own glory, and then reveals, slowly and magnificently, that these two things are not in conflict. They are the same thing.

The Twist That Changes Everything

Here is where it turns.

The God who does not need you, chose you. The story that was never about you, included you. Not as a footnote. As a son. As a daughter. Purchased, not improved. Adopted, not auditioned.

John Piper has spent a lifetime saying it one way: God is most glorified in us when we are most satisfied in Him. That sentence only makes sense if you understand that His glory comes first and your satisfaction is the fruit of it, not the root.

The cross was not God helping you reach your potential. It was God satisfying His justice and His love at the same time, in the same place, at the greatest possible cost. You were not the architect of that plan. You were the beneficiary of it.

So Come to the Book Differently

Do not come asking what this means for you. Come asking what this says about Him. Come asking what this moment in the story reveals about the character of the God who wrote it.

The Psalms will still comfort you. Proverbs will still instruct you. The promises of God will still hold you in the dark. But they will do all of that with far more power when you understand that they were not assembled for your convenience. They were breathed out by a God who had His own glory in mind, and who, in the most generous act in the history of everything, decided that His glory and your salvation would be the same story.

The Bible was never about you.

And you have never been more loved.
